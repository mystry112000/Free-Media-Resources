# 🎬 Free Stock Videos

Pre-recorded video clips anyone can download and use. Perfect for website backgrounds, YouTube videos, social media reels, and ads.

---

## 1. ⭐ Coverr

**🔗 https://coverr.co**

Beautiful videos specifically made for website backgrounds and hero sections.

- **Library:** 10,000+ videos
- **Best for:** Website backgrounds, hero sections, video banners, social media clips
- **Special:** Search by mood and category

**License:** ✅ Free for commercial & personal use. No attribution needed.

---

## 2. ⭐ Pexels Videos

**🔗 https://www.pexels.com/videos**

The video section of Pexels — same great quality as their photos.

- **Library:** 1+ million videos
- **Best for:** Marketing videos, social media content, website backgrounds, ads

**License:** ✅ Free for commercial & personal use. No attribution needed.

---

## 3. ⭐ Mixkit

**🔗 https://mixkit.co**

Premium-quality free video site. Also has free templates for Premiere Pro and After Effects.

- **Library:** 10,000+ assets
- **Best for:** Professional videos, video templates, motion graphics, intros
- **Also has:** Free music and sound effects

**License:** ✅ Free for commercial use. No attribution needed.

---

## 4. ⭐ Pixabay Videos

**🔗 https://pixabay.com/videos**

The video section of Pixabay — free videos with the same easy-to-use license.

- **Best for:** Quick finds, variety, supplementary footage

**License:** ✅ Free for commercial use. No attribution needed.

---

## 5. ⭐ Mazwai

**🔗 https://mazwai.com**

Cinematic, artistic video footage curated by real filmmakers.

- **Best for:** Artistic projects, cinematic content, high-end productions

**License:** ✅ Free for commercial use. Attribution to the filmmaker required.

---

## 6. ⭐ Videvo

**🔗 https://www.videvo.net**

Professional stock video website with both free and premium clips.

- **Best for:** Professional video projects, commercial use

**License:** ✅ Free tier with attribution to Videvo.

---

## 💡 Tips for Beginners

- Start with **Coverr** or **Pexels Videos** — easiest to use
- Use **Mixkit** if you need professional-looking footage
- Most videos come in **MP4 format** (works everywhere)
- Check the resolution before downloading (1080p is standard)
- For website backgrounds, choose **loop-friendly** videos
